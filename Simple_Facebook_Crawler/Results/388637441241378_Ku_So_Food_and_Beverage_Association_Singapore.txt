The 20th Singapore Food Festival

Back for the 20th year, the Singapore Food Festival is back with a month-long of islandwide activities. Celebrating the evolution of Singapore’s culinary journey throughout the years, the festival will kick off with a range of specially-crafted menus to creative cooking classes and food-themed arts and entertainment. There will also be a line-up of classes and workshops where participants will have the opportunity to whip up dishes with renowned chefs. With an increasingly diverse palate, Singapore’s love for food is illustrated by an ever growing pool of local talent making quite an impression on the international culinary platform. The festival kicks off from 28 June to 28 July.

ACRA Reports That at Least Two New Restaurants Open Daily in Singapore

A recent report from the Accounting and Corporate Regulatory Authority (ACRA) calculated that an average of two restaurants open in Singapore daily. According to Colliers International, there was a 8.1% growth in the number of F&B establishments, from 5,969 to 6,453 within a two-year period. The figures did not include new establishments in 2012. A total of 537 restaurants were closed in the same year.
